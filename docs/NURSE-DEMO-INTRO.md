# Kairos — A Quick Note Before You Click

Hey —

Thanks for taking five minutes to look at this. I know you just got off shift (or you're trying to), and the last thing you want is one more tab open. I'll keep it short.

## What this is

Kairos is a working prototype I've been building on the side. It's a card-based nurse workstation that sits on top of an Epic-shaped inbox. Right now it's demo-only — every patient, every message, every result you'll see is simulated from real shift workflows here at this clinic. Nothing is connected to live Epic. Nothing leaves my laptop.

## Why I built it

You know the morning. You open the In Basket and check Results, then Results Follow-Up filtered to your provider, then Rx Request filtered, then Patient Call, then Pt Advice Request, then the custom "addressed to me" search you built because Epic's standard view shows the pool, not you. Six filtered views, every morning, before you've touched a single patient.

And the counts lie. The sidebar says **27/31** in Results Follow-Up. Nine of those are actually yours. Across all six boxes the sidebar implies fifty-something items of work. The real number is closer to fifteen. I've worked the In Basket enough to say this clearly: **I cannot answer the question "how much work do I have today" using Epic.** That bothers me. I built Kairos because I wanted to answer it.

## What you're about to see

Six cards. Each one is a real pattern I observed on shift this week — a results follow-up with a new TTE order, a lipid-panel education message, a Crestor dose change, a phone-only echo result, a multi-stage callback investigation, and a denial cascade across eight days. Things you'll recognize.

The tour auto-plays. You don't have to do anything but watch. End-to-end it runs about four to five minutes.

## What I'm asking from you

Honest reactions, in any order:

- Did anything look wrong clinically?
- Did the messages sound like something you'd actually send to a patient?
- What patterns are missing — what do you handle every day that isn't here?
- Any safety concerns? Anything that made you flinch?

If something is off, please say so. That's the whole point of asking nurses first.

## What this is NOT

Not replacing nurses. Not a tool that types for the provider. Not connected to live Epic. Not collecting any patient data — there is no backend, no database, no PHI. The URL is a localhost demo. When I close the laptop, it's gone.

> You stay the nurse. Kairos just stops making you the database.

## Closing

I built this because I got tired of waiting for software that was made for us. The vendors aren't going to do it — they don't sit in our chairs. So I'm trying. I want feedback from the people who'd actually have to live with this thing, before anyone who isn't a nurse sees it. If something here is wrong, I'd rather hear it from you than learn it later.

Thanks for looking.

— Brandon
